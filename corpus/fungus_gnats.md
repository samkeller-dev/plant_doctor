# Fungus Gnats

Fungus gnats (family Sciaridae) are small dark flies that breed in consistently moist potting soil. The adults are a nuisance but harmless; the larvae feed on fungi, decaying organic matter, and — when populations are high — fine root hairs of seedlings and stressed plants.

## Identification

- Adults: 2–4 mm, dark gray to black, slow weak fliers, often seen running across the soil surface or on nearby walls.
- Larvae: translucent white with a black head, up to ~5 mm, in the top 1–2 cm of soil.
- A yellow sticky card placed at the soil surface catches adults and confirms infestation.
- Adults are easily confused with fruit flies; fruit flies are tan/red, hover near food, and have red eyes.

## Why they appear

The single biggest predictor is persistently wet topsoil. Overwatering, peat-heavy mixes that stay damp, and pots without drainage create ideal conditions. Indoor populations rarely originate spontaneously; they usually arrive in newly purchased plants or in fresh bagged potting mix.

## Treatment — break the life cycle

The egg-to-adult cycle is roughly 3–4 weeks at room temperature. Sustained suppression for one full cycle clears most infestations.

1. **Let the top 1–2 inches of soil dry between waterings.** This kills eggs and most larvae. For thirsty plants, bottom-water so the surface stays dry.
2. **BTI (***Bacillus thuringiensis* israelensis**)** drenches — sold as mosquito bits or dunks — kill larvae specifically and are safe for plants and people. Apply weekly for 3–4 weeks.
3. **Sticky cards** at the soil surface trap adults and reduce egg-laying. Keep them in place for the full treatment window.
4. **Top-dress with ~1 cm of horticultural sand, fine gravel, or a layer of perlite** to deny adults a place to lay eggs in the moist soil below.
5. For stubborn cases, a hydrogen peroxide drench (1 part 3% H₂O₂ to 4 parts water) kills larvae on contact; use sparingly as it can stress roots.

## Do not

Do not rely on adult-targeting sprays alone — they leave the larval reservoir intact and the population rebounds.
